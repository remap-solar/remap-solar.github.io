Phil-LiDAR 2: Nationwide Detailed Resources Mapping using LiDAR

The Phil-LiDAR 2 Program is a DOST-funded research program aimed at producing methodologies and maps for the detailed assessment of resources -- agricultural, coastal, forest, hydrologic, and renewable energy -- in the Philippines using LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) and other Geospatial technologies.

Philippine Renewable Energy Resource Mapping from LiDAR Surveys (REMap)

The Philipine Renewable Energy Resource Mapping from LiDAR Surveys (REMap) Project is one of the component projects of Phil-LiDAR 2. Together with its partner SUCs (State University and Colleges) and HEIs (Higher Education Institutions), its objective is to map the biomass, hydro, solar, and wind renewable energy resources in the country and determine the most suitable sites for renewable energy power plant development.

SOLAR Component

The Solar component of REMap is tasked with modelling the solar energy resource in the Philippines and selecting suitable sites for solar PV farms.

For solar radiation modelling, the GRASS module r.sun is used.
